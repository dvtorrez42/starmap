How to Use the Star Map

  1. Find your location — type a city name in the search box or enter latitude and longitude manually. You can also click Use my location on a supported browser.
  2. Set the date and time — choose any date and time, or click Use current time to see the sky right now.
  3. Render the sky — click Render Sky to generate your star map. The circular chart is centred on your zenith, with north at the top and the horizon at the edge.
  4. Explore — hover over any star to see its name, magnitude, altitude, and azimuth. Toggle constellation lines, star names, altitude grid, and the Milky Way glow from the Display panel.
  5. Export (optional) — click Export PDF to download a printable A4 sky chart with your own title and subtitle.

Frequently Asked Questions

What stars are visible tonight from my location?

Type your city in the search box, set the date and time to tonight, and click Render Sky. The map shows every naked-eye star above your horizon, sized and coloured by brightness and spectral type. Stars brighter than magnitude 2 are labelled by name.

How do I identify constellations in the night sky?

Enable Constellation lines and Constellation labels in the Display panel. The star map draws the official IAU pattern for each constellation and labels it with its standard three-letter abbreviation — for example Ori for Orion, UMa for Ursa Major, and Sco for Scorpius.

Can I see the sky for a past or future date?

Yes. The date and time fields accept any value, so you can view the night sky on your birthday, a historic event, or any future date. Positions are calculated mathematically rather than fetched from a live service, so there is no limit on the date range.

How accurate is this star map?

Star positions come from the HYG star catalogue (a combination of the Hipparcos, Yale Bright Star, and Gliese catalogues). Coordinates are converted from equatorial to horizontal using standard formulae for local sidereal time, accurate to well within a degree for naked-eye observing.

Can I print a star chart for a specific date as a gift?

Yes. Render the sky for the date and location you want, then click Export PDF. You can add a custom title — such as a name or occasion — and a subtitle with the date and place. The download is a dark-themed A4 PDF ready to print or frame.

Which constellations are visible from the Northern Hemisphere tonight?

That depends on your latitude, the date, and the time. Enter your location and today's date to find out — the star map will show exactly which constellations are currently above your horizon and where in the sky each one sits.
